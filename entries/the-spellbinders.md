## Career

- The Spellbinders were a Jersey City soul and doo-wop group, formed in the mid-1960s by McArthur Munford with James Wright, Bobby Shivers, Ben Grant, and Elouise Pennington
- Signed to Columbia Records in 1965 under producer Van McCoy, they charted with “For You” and “Help Me (Get Myself Back Together Again),” with “Chain Reaction” and “Help Me” hitting in the UK; their album The Magic of the Spellbinders is regarded as a soul classic
- They toured extensively with Jackie Wilson, Joe Tex, and Wilson Pickett before breaking up by 1969
