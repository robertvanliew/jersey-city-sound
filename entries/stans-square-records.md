## Career

- Stan's Square Records was a Jersey City record store founded by Stan Krause in 1965 in Journal Square, later moving to 349 Bergen Avenue
- For five decades the store was a cornerstone for music lovers and record diggers, known especially for preserving and promoting acappella, soul, and rhythm and blues
- The store sold mixtapes to the whole community from the city's top mixtape DJs, including DJ 007, DJ Mad Money, DJ E Double, DJ NEL E NEL, DJ Wimpy B, DJ Big Time, DJ Dyce, DJ Ju-Town, DJ DX, DJ Wizard, DJ Madden, DJ Walley Katt & X5, Semaj da DJ, and many more
- Krause founded Catamount Records (1964), the label that launched the recording careers of vocal groups including The Persuasions and 14 Karat Soul; Frank Zappa first heard The Persuasions singing over the phone from the store, where Krause was the group's manager
- Krause founded the United in Group Harmony Association (UGHA) and produced music and live vocal-harmony concerts
- In 2015, Jersey City Mayor Steven Fulop issued a proclamation for the store's 50th anniversary honoring Krause as 'Stan the Music Man of Jersey City'; in 2016 he received the Jersey City Living Legend Award
- Krause died on May 20, 2016, at age 74; the store closed in 2018 following his passing
