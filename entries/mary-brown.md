## Career

- Mary Brown is a Grammy-nominated singer, songwriter, and producer from Jersey City, New Jersey — among the most decorated writers of the modern R&B era, with 15× RIAA Platinum and 6× RIAA Gold certified credits, a two-time Grammy nominee best known for penning Destiny's Child's first Billboard No. 1, “No, No, No”
- Operatically trained, she won the Governor's Award at the New Jersey Performing Arts Center competition as a teenager, earning a full scholarship to study music at Rutgers University
- During her first year of college she signed to Reprise Records as one-third of Teddy Riley's girl-group trio Abstrac'; the group's single “Right and Hype” spent six weeks on the Billboard Hot 100 and reached No. 23 on the Hot Black Singles chart across a 17-week run
- She went on to session and background work for artists including Brandy, Bobby Brown, Guy, and fellow New Jersey native Whitney Houston
- Her songwriting credits include Destiny's Child's international No. 1 “No, No, No,” work for Jaheim, Day26's debut single “Got Me Goin’,” and Danity Kane's “Bad Girl” (featuring Missy Elliott) and “Ecstasy” (featuring Rick Ross) — the last two written on MTV's Making the Band 3, where Sean “Diddy” Combs praised her “mean pen game”
- She contributed to Michael Jackson's final studio album, Invincible (2001), and has worked with Mary J. Blige, Wyclef Jean, Queen Latifah, Usher, SWV, Patti LaBelle, Diana Ross, and LL Cool J; her “No, No, No” was later sampled by Jack Harlow on his Grammy-nominated Come Home the Kids Miss You (2022)
- Her honors include Soul Train's Lady of Soul Award and four BMI Publisher & Songwriter Awards; she co-chaired the Recording Academy's New York Songwriters & Composers Wing (2022–2023), runs the publishing company Ms. Mary's Music, and works as an educator and philanthropist
- She is a granddaughter of Kathryn Van Liew and, with her brother Cool Sir Brown, one of the family's modern hitmakers; she is the niece of soul singer McArthur Munford of the Spellbinders — her mother, Lorraine H. Brown, was Munford's sister — placing her among the cornerstone figures of the Van Liew musical dynasty alongside Chill Divine (Gary Van Liew) and DJ DX (Robert Van Liew)
