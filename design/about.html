    About the Archive

    Every voice in the city, on the record.

    The Jersey City Sound is the encyclopedia-archive of Jersey City's music culture — its DJs, artists, producers, venues, crews, and the records they left behind. It was founded on July 5, 2026 to do one thing: make sure the scene's history is written down, cited, and permanent.

    Why This Exists§

    Jersey City's music history lives mostly in memory — in tapes that changed hands at record-store counters, in club rooms that closed, in footage on aging channels. When the people who hold that memory pass, the record goes with them. This archive documents it while the sources are still here to be asked.

    Methodology§

    Every entry follows the same rules, regardless of who it is about:

    Cite or cut. Verifiable claims only. Each entry carries numbered sources — recordings, press, film, or documented archive research. Where the record is thin, the entry says so plainly and lists what is wanted.

    Neutral voice. Museum-label tone: factual, warm, zero hype. Numbers and receipts speak — streams, credits, years — not adjectives.

    Everyone gets documented accurately. The archive documents the whole scene by the same standard, founder included.

    Contribution, not self-description. No one is published for calling themselves a legend, for follower counts, or for asking. Entries document what someone verifiably did — a release, a credit, a flyer, a room they held down. Names the record cannot yet support are held in a private research queue until evidence arrives. The archive's power comes from what it refuses to publish as much as what it preserves.

    Corrections & Contributions§

    The archive is built to be corrected. We welcome leads, memories, photographs, flyers, recordings, and corrections — but publication requires verifiable evidence; every entry is researched, cited, and reviewed before it joins the record. If an entry has an error, a missing credit, or a fact you can back with a receipt, use the suggest-an-edit form — every entry page links to it. Charted entries also carry a cryptographic seal: each is signed with the archive’s key so anyone can verify that the page and its cited facts are unchanged since sealing. For anything else, reach the archive at contact@jerseycitysound.com.

    The Permanence Commitment§

    Archives fail when they depend on one person or one server. This one is built not to: the site is static and mirrorable, its content is licensed for reuse, snapshots are submitted to the Internet Archive, and stewardship is arranged beyond the founder. If this domain ever disappears, the record survives.
