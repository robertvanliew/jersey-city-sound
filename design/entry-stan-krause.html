    Stan Krause

    Record producer · Record store owner · Label founder · 1964 to 2016

    Acappella Soul R&B 1964 to 2016

    Stan Krause was the Jersey City record store owner, record producer, and label founder behind Stan's Square Records and Catamount Records, a central figure in the city's acappella and rhythm-and-blues history.

    The Persuasions
    14 Karat Soul

    As producer and label owner, Krause broke The Persuasions (No. 16 R&B, 1972) and 14 Karat Soul (Saturday Night Live, 1983), pushing Jersey City street-corner harmony onto the national stage.

    Sealed in the Record · 2026-07-13 · verify

    In the Record§

    He founded Catamount Records in 1964 and opened Stan's Square Records in 1965, and the label launched the recording careers of vocal groups including The Persuasions and 14 Karat Soul.

    Frank Zappa first heard The Persuasions singing over the phone from Krause's store, where Krause managed the group and recorded their first live tracks.

    Krause's acts reached the national stage: The Persuasions charted on Billboard (Street Corner Symphony, No. 16 R&B and No. 88 pop, 1972), and 14 Karat Soul performed on Saturday Night Live in 1983 and drew large audiences in Japan.

    He founded the United in Group Harmony Association (UGHA) and produced live vocal-harmony concerts for decades under the banner Classic Harmony Productions.

    In 2015, Jersey City Mayor Steven Fulop honored him with a proclamation as “Stan the Music Man of Jersey City,” and in 2016 he received the Jersey City Living Legend Award.

    Krause died on May 20, 2016, at the age of 74, and his store closed in 2018.
